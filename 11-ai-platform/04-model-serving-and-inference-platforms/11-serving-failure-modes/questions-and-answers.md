### SERVING-FAILURE-MODES-JN-01

- [ ] **Question:** What is Out-of-memory errors, and why does it matter in Serving failure modes?
> **Covered in:** [Serving failure modes — Out-of-memory errors](README.md#out-of-memory-errors)

**Answer:** **Definition.** The term Out-of-memory errors refers to an AI lifecycle mechanism that affects the identity, quality, safety, performance or cost of a data, model or runtime revision within Serving failure modes. **Mechanism.** A process reserves virtual address ranges and faults pages in when it first touches them. Page tables translate virtual pages to physical frames; anonymous pages hold heap and stack data, file-backed pages participate in the page cache, and shared mappings can point several processes at the same frames. Reclaim drops clean cache first, writes dirty pages when necessary and may swap eligible anonymous pages before an OOM policy selects a victim. Its guarantee applies only at its own boundary, so upstream identity, downstream dependencies and asynchronous convergence still matter. **Evidence and failure behavior.** Healthy evidence separates virtual size, resident set, shared/file-backed cache, page-fault rate, reclaim, swap activity, PSI stall time and cgroup events. A low `free` number is not automatically failure because cache is reclaimable; sustained major faults, swap-in/out, direct reclaim, growing RSS with stable workload, OOM events or remote NUMA access explain real latency and termination risk. Compare those signals with desired configuration, workload shape and a known healthy baseline before choosing a mitigation. **Trade-off.** The choice normally balances simplicity, isolation, performance, recovery effort, portability and cost; moving a boundary solves one problem while shifting another to the caller or operator. **Production example.** A team operating a service in which Out-of-memory errors matters records the workload and configuration baseline, injects one bounded failure in a sandbox, alerts on the first user-impact or saturation signal, and verifies recovery through the original Serving failure modes journey rather than an administrative green status alone.
