### AWS-GLOBAL-ACCELERATOR-JN-01

- [ ] **Question:** What is Anycast IP, and why does it matter in AWS Global Accelerator?
> **Covered in:** [AWS Global Accelerator — Anycast IP](README.md#anycast-ip)

**Answer:** **Definition.** The same static addresses advertise globally and enter a nearby AWS edge. **Mechanism.** The mechanism receives configuration or runtime input, validates preconditions, changes or derives state, and exposes status to the next component. Synchronous parts return a direct outcome; asynchronous parts need durable status, reconciliation and idempotency because the original caller may disappear. Its guarantee applies only at its own boundary, so upstream identity, downstream dependencies and asynchronous convergence still matter. **Evidence and failure behavior.** Healthy evidence shows the expected state transition and its owner, timestamp and reason. Invalid input, stale state, conflicting writers, hidden limits and dependencies that partially complete are the most useful failure categories to distinguish. Compare those signals with desired configuration, workload shape and a known healthy baseline before choosing a mitigation. **Trade-off.** The choice normally balances simplicity, isolation, performance, recovery effort, portability and cost; moving a boundary solves one problem while shifting another to the caller or operator. **Production example.** A team operating a service in which Anycast IP matters records the workload and configuration baseline, injects one bounded failure in a sandbox, alerts on the first user-impact or saturation signal, and verifies recovery through the original AWS Global Accelerator journey rather than an administrative green status alone.

### AWS-GLOBAL-ACCELERATOR-JN-03

- [ ] **Question:** What is Endpoint group, and why does it matter in AWS Global Accelerator?

**Answer:** **Definition.** Regional endpoints, traffic dial and health/check settings. **Mechanism.** A real request crosses several independent states: name resolution returns an address, the source selects a route and source address, link or overlay forwarding reaches the next hop, stateful or stateless policy evaluates the flow, transport establishes communication, and TLS/application protocols negotiate their own contract. ### AWS-GLOBAL-ACCELERATOR-JN-05

- [ ] **Question:** What is Health failover, and why does it matter in AWS Global Accelerator?
> **Covered in:** [AWS Global Accelerator — Health failover](README.md#health-failover)

**Answer:** **Definition.** Unhealthy endpoints/regions are removed under detection thresholds and state implications. **Mechanism.** A caller assigns a deadline and attempt budget, classifies an error, applies bounded backoff with jitter only when retry is safe, and propagates capacity limits upstream. Circuit breakers, bulkheads and load shedding prevent one dependency from consuming every worker; recovery probes and gradual traffic return avoid a second overload. Its guarantee applies only at its own boundary, so upstream identity, downstream dependencies and asynchronous convergence still matter. **Evidence and failure behavior.** Healthy evidence shows bounded queues, stable retry ratios and recovery within an objective. Unbounded retries, identical timeouts at every layer, non-idempotent replays, shared resource pools and failover to an unqualified target commonly turn a small fault into an outage. Compare those signals with desired configuration, workload shape and a known healthy baseline before choosing a mitigation. **Trade-off.** The choice normally balances simplicity, isolation, performance, recovery effort, portability and cost; moving a boundary solves one problem while shifting another to the caller or operator. **Production example.** A team operating a service in which Health failover matters records the workload and configuration baseline, injects one bounded failure in a sandbox, alerts on the first user-impact or saturation signal, and verifies recovery through the original AWS Global Accelerator journey rather than an administrative green status alone.
