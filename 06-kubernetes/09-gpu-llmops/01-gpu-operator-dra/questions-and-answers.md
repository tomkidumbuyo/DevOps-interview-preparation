### GPU-OPERATOR-DEVICE-PLUGINS-AND-DRA-JN-01

- [ ] **Question:** What is Driver, and why does it matter in GPU Operator, device plugins and DRA?
> **Covered in:** [GPU Operator, device plugins and DRA — Driver](README.md#driver)

**Answer:** **Definition.** Host kernel module/firmware is the foundation and must match hardware/kernel/runtime. **Mechanism.** The mechanism receives configuration or runtime input, validates preconditions, changes or derives state, and exposes status to the next component. Synchronous parts return a direct outcome; asynchronous parts need durable status, reconciliation and idempotency because the original caller may disappear. Its guarantee applies only at its own boundary, so upstream identity, downstream dependencies and asynchronous convergence still matter. **Evidence and failure behavior.** Healthy evidence shows the expected state transition and its owner, timestamp and reason. Invalid input, stale state, conflicting writers, hidden limits and dependencies that partially complete are the most useful failure categories to distinguish. Compare those signals with desired configuration, workload shape and a known healthy baseline before choosing a mitigation. **Trade-off.** The choice normally balances simplicity, isolation, performance, recovery effort, portability and cost; moving a boundary solves one problem while shifting another to the caller or operator. **Production example.** A team operating a service in which Driver matters records the workload and configuration baseline, injects one bounded failure in a sandbox, alerts on the first user-impact or saturation signal, and verifies recovery through the original GPU Operator, device plugins and DRA journey rather than an administrative green status alone.
