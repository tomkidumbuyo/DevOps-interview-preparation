- [ ] **Question:** What is User research, and why does it matter in Platform product management?
> **Covered in:** [Platform product management — User research](README.md#user-research)

**Answer:** **Definition.** The term User research refers to a platform-product mechanism that turns a user intent into a governed, supported and observable capability within Platform product management. **Mechanism.** An asset or service receives an owner, lifecycle state, data/risk class, policy, budget and evidence requirements. Usage is attributed to stable organizational dimensions, compared with outcome and SLO, and reviewed through changes, exceptions and retirement; automated guardrails enforce the decisions at the relevant control point. Its guarantee applies only at its own boundary, so upstream identity, downstream dependencies and asynchronous convergence still matter. **Evidence and failure behavior.** Healthy evidence connects an accountable owner and approved intent to runtime inventory, audit and unit cost. Orphaned resources, permanent exceptions, unallocated shared cost, vanity utilization and savings that damage reliability or quality are common failure modes. Compare those signals with desired configuration, workload shape and a known healthy baseline before choosing a mitigation. **Trade-off.** The choice normally balances simplicity, isolation, performance, recovery effort, portability and cost; moving a boundary solves one problem while shifting another to the caller or operator. **Production example.** A team operating a service in which User research matters records the workload and configuration baseline, injects one bounded failure in a sandbox, alerts on the first user-impact or saturation signal, and verifies recovery through the original Platform product management journey rather than an administrative green status alone.
