### TLS-AND-CERTIFICATES-JN-03

- [ ] **Question:** What is Certificate authorities, and why does it matter in TLS and certificates?
> **Covered in:** [TLS and certificates — Certificate authorities](README.md#certificate-authorities)

**Answer:** **Definition.** The term Certificate authorities is a trust-control mechanism that identifies an actor or protected asset and enforces which action is allowed under explicit context, with audit, rotation, revocation and recovery behavior within TLS and certificates. **Mechanism.** At runtime a caller presents or derives an identity, the enforcement point gathers identity, resource and request context, and applicable rules produce allow or deny. The effective decision is the intersection of all guardrails; encryption protects bytes but does not replace authorization, and audit records explain which decision was made. Its guarantee applies only at its own boundary, so upstream identity, downstream dependencies and asynchronous convergence still matter. **Evidence and failure behavior.** Healthy evidence includes a short-lived attributable identity, narrowly scoped access and an audit event for the intended resource.
