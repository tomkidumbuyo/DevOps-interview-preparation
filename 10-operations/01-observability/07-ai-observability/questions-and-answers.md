### AI-OBSERVABILITY-JN-01

- [ ] **Question:** What is Prompt and response metadata, and why does it matter in AI observability?
> **Covered in:** [AI observability — Prompt and response metadata](README.md#prompt-and-response-metadata)

**Answer:** **Definition.** The term Prompt and response metadata is an AI data or behavior artifact whose exact revision, inputs, transformations, compatibility and evaluation evidence affect the result produced at runtime within AI observability. **Mechanism.** Inputs are validated and transformed, a versioned model or pipeline performs work, and post-processing and policy produce the exposed result. Offline evaluation compares a candidate with a baseline; shadow or canary traffic supplies production evidence; lineage connects the decision back to exact artifacts and data. Its guarantee applies only at its own boundary, so upstream identity, downstream dependencies and asynchronous convergence still matter. **Evidence and failure behavior.** Healthy evidence combines task quality and safety with latency, token or accelerator work, failure rate and unit cost. Training-serving skew, stale or unauthorized retrieval, incompatible runtime/hardware, biased evaluators, prompt injection and silent provider/model changes are core production risks. Compare those signals with desired configuration, workload shape and a known healthy baseline before choosing a mitigation. **Trade-off.** The choice normally balances simplicity, isolation, performance, recovery effort, portability and cost; moving a boundary solves one problem while shifting another to the caller or operator. **Production example.** A team operating a service in which Prompt and response metadata matters records the workload and configuration baseline, injects one bounded failure in a sandbox, alerts on the first user-impact or saturation signal, and verifies recovery through the original AI observability journey rather than an administrative green status alone.
